Forging Forward: A Capstone Master Plan for the Redevelopment of the Former US Steel Site in Duluth, MN

US Steel: Duluth Works Site History

The US Steel Duluth Works steel plant operated from 1915-1979 on the western edge of the city of Duluth, MN. Pictured here in an historic image of the original plant is the material storage area where large quantities of iron ore, coal, and other raw materials would rest before the steel prodcution process. Today, the only architectural relic that remains onsite in memory of former steel plant are the concrete pillings that sorround the material storage yard. Unfortunately the Closure of the plant took with it many Duluthian livelihoods as it was the city's #1 employer. Today teh site sits idle, deemed a US Superfund and briwnfield site in 2003. Roughly 30 acres of onsite heavy metal contamination have restricted community use and redeveloment of the site until present.

Recovery + Redevelopment

The US Steel site sized at roughly 500 acres is one of the largest US brownfield cleanups sites in the nation. An evironmental imapct study was completed in August of 2015 and an estimated 700,000 cubic yards of contaiminated soils are scheduled to be excavated and caped in place. The City of Duluth Planning Auth. as well as area residents are hoping to redevelop the site in an effort to bring people back to the historically and culutrally significant shoreline in addition to balanicng new jobs and industry.

Design Approach

Based on currrent market conditions and slow pollution cleanup efforts, the redevelopment of the US Steel site will need to be phased over the course of a decade or more. As such, a unqiue opportunity exists to restore community and culutral value back to the landscpae. A design approach to embracing the concept of industrial heritage inspires a productive landscape nursery in the materil storage yard. The remaining architectural relic, the crumbling ore wall gives material reference and rhythm to the space. Additionally, a landscape library and processing building serves as a reception and gathering points for residents hoping to connect with the histroic shoreline and wild rice areas.
